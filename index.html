The consoles war

Games releases

A first interesting metric is the amount of games released for each console over the years. We've grouped those by manufacturers :

Atari was first in the console industry but died quicky (will it come back ?). Then arrived Nintendo, Sony and only later Microsoft. We can observe a peak of game release around year 2009, mainly due to the amount of games for mobile consoles such as Nintendo's DS and Sony's PSP but also because of the success of the Wii.

This peak is followed by a collapse which is essentially because of the vanishing of those mobile consoles (maybe because of the developement of smartphones games ?) despite the efforts of Nintendo with the release of Nintendo 3DS.

However we can also see that the production of games for the Wii and its sucessor the WiiU became very small. Also there is a general decrease in video games releases for other consoles such as the X360, PS3 or PC. All those together created this collapse in video game releases.

Sales evolution

A second metric we can consider is of course the number of sales. This graph shows how many sales were made by the games released in a given year. We can see that Microsoft Xbox is essentially present only in North America and Europe, while Nintendo dominates the sales in Japan. In other countries it's mainly Sony making most of the sales.

The genreal worldwide trend follows the same evolution as the previous graph, once again this collapse in sales might be explained by the arrival of smartphones games (which are not taken into account in our dataset).

Sales per genres

At last, it might be also pertinent to ask ourself which video game genre is the most present on each platform. On this graph you can select the genre you're interested in to get a better idea of which console sells the most this genre.

For instance, most of the sport games were sold on the Wii which is not surprising. Indeed this category has been one of the Wii's main target as Nintendo's launch title Wii Sports reminds us. When it comes to puzzle games it's Nintendo's DS that made most of the sales, surely because its stylus-driven interface and touchscreen made it ideal for this kind of game.

Concerning shooter games, it's clearly the Playstations and Xbox that dominate the sales for this genre. And as a last example, in strategy games playing require a lot of rapid clicks on many parts of the screen which makes nothing better than a mouse for that. This surely explains why most of strategy games sales are made on PC.
